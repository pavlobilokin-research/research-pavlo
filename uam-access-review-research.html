Context
Why access reviews matter
Access reviews are a core control in any identity governance program. Without them, permissions accumulate silently — employees change roles, contractors finish engagements, and orphaned accounts sit open indefinitely. A quarterly cadence is the industry standard for most SaaS environments, with monthly sweeps recommended for high-risk systems.

Key principle: Access review is not about catching bad actors. It's about correcting the natural drift that happens as people change roles, teams restructure, and systems get added.

Stakeholder Communication
What to send and when
Manager certification email
  • Send on Day 1 of Week 2
  • Include direct link to certification list
  • 5-business-day deadline, clearly stated
  • Keep it under 100 words — managers skim
  • Day 4 chase if no response
Escalation triggers
  • Admin access uncertified after 5 days → manager's manager
  • No response to 2 chasers → HR loop-in
  • Leaver account still active → immediate CISO flag
  • Exception request → written sign-off required
Pre-review Checklist
Before you start each cycle
  • Confirm systems in scope with security lead
  • Pull latest HR leavers list from People team
  • Extract user lists from all in-scope systems
  • Prepare manager certification spreadsheet or tool
  • Draft and schedule manager comms email
  • Set calendar reminders for SLA checkpoints
  • Confirm audit evidence storage location is ready
  • Brief any UAM team members involved in the cycle
